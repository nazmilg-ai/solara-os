Subsequent Product Specifications shall follow this structure unless superseded by an approved architectural change.

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the Roller Blind implementation of the Sales MVP Product Engine.

It implements the architecture defined in MCD-01A. It shall not redefine any Shared Platform Service. Where shared functionality exists, this document references MCD-01A and defines only the Roller-specific implementation.

## 3. Construction Variants

**Terminology note:** "Construction Variant" (used throughout this document) is this specification's name for the concept MCD-01A §8 calls "Product Variants" — genuine construction differences only, never Material/Fabric attributes. The terms are equivalent; future MCD-01B-xx specifications may use either term consistently but shall not treat them as distinct concepts.

**Standard Roller Blind** — open roller system, standard brackets, optional fascia (where supported), optional accessories.

**Cassette Roller Blind** — fully enclosed cassette system: different headrail, different brackets, different assembly, different manufacturing process, different pricing, different installation considerations.

Construction differences only. Material selection shall not create additional construction variants.

**Cassette vs. accessory (resolves prior ambiguity):** Cassette is a Construction/System, not an Accessory. The test for an accessory is whether it can be added or removed without changing the underlying construction of the product (e.g. a remote, chain tensioner, or solar panel all attach to an existing product without altering it). A cassette fails this test — it changes the headrail, bracket design, assembly, appearance, and sometimes the fabric deduction and tube selection. Cassette shall therefore only ever appear as a Construction Variant (this section) and must never also appear as an Accessory Engine group.

- Window Drop
- Control Side
- Installation Height
- Roll Direction

## 6. Measurement Rules

Measurement behaviour is provided by the shared Measurement Engine. Roller-specific rules include:
- Width
- Drop
- Recess measurement
- Exact measurement
- Reverse roll clearance
- Window handle clearance
- Cassette clearance
- Bracket projection
- Tube size implications

Supplier maximum dimensions are defined by Supplier Capability documents.

**Extension bracket owning engine (resolves prior ambiguity):** whether an extension bracket becomes mandatory is an Accessory Engine rule (MCD-01A §5.8, "Mandatory accessories"), not a Measurement Engine rule — it consumes drop/recess measurement output but is evaluated and enforced by the Accessory Engine. See §11.

## 7. Validation Rules

The shared Validation Engine performs validation. Roller-specific validation includes:
- Minimum width
- Maximum width
- Minimum drop
- Maximum drop
- Maximum area
- Tube compatibility
- Bracket compatibility
- Cassette compatibility
- Motor compatibility
- Fabric compatibility
- Fabric railroading/joining requirement (where width exceeds standard fabric roll width)
- Cassette / reverse-roll compatibility (whether reverse roll is available on the Cassette Construction Variant)
- Weight limits

Supplier-specific values are not defined here.

**Not yet specified — needs supplier/business data before technical design:** minimum drop value(s), the fabric roll width threshold that triggers railroading/joining and its pricing/lead-time consequence, and whether reverse roll is available on Cassette (§3) or Standard only. These are real Roller Blind constraints per the specification review and must be populated — with actual values, not left as category headings — before implementation.

## 8. Material & Fabric

Material selection is independent from Construction Variant. Customer-facing material labels include:
- Light Filtering
- Dimout
- Blackout
- Screen
- Fire Retardant (FR)
- Moisture Resistant
- Sustainable

These labels are Material/Fabric attributes. They shall never create Product Variants.

## 9. Operation

Operation behaviour is defined by the shared Operation Engine.

**Roller supports:**
- Manual
- Manual or Motorised (Advisor Selection)

**Roller does not support:**
- Manual or Motorised (Factory Selection)
- Motorised Only

Operation modes shall not be redefined.

## 10. Motor Implementation

Motor compatibility is determined by the shared Motor Engine. Compatible motor families are determined by the Supplier Capability Library — refer to the applicable Supplier Capability document for supported motor systems.

Motor selection depends upon: Supplier, blind dimensions, blind weight, tube size, customer requirements, power availability.

Motor compatibility shall not be hard-coded.

## 11. Accessories

The shared Accessory Engine manages compatibility. Roller accessory groups include:
- Universal Brackets
- Top Fix Brackets
- Face Fix Brackets
- Extension Brackets
- Fascia
- Bottom Bars
- Side Channels
- Chain Tensioners
- Child Safety Devices
- Remote Controls
- Wall Switches
- Smart Hubs
- Solar Panels
- Chargers
- Battery Packs

**Bracket Type reconciliation:** Bracket Type (§5) is the Configuration-layer selection. The specific bracket products listed above (Universal, Top Fix, Face Fix, Extension) are Accessory Engine items; which of them are eligible is determined by the Bracket Type selection together with the chosen Construction Variant (§3). This satisfies the Construction/System vs. Accessories test in MCD-01A §3 — swapping bracket type does not change the underlying roller construction, so it correctly sits in Configuration/Accessories, unlike Cassette.

Supplier documents determine availability.

Extension Brackets become a mandatory accessory when drop/recess measurements (§6) exceed supplier-defined thresholds — see Supplier Capability documents for the specific values per supplier and tube system.

## 12. Child Safety

The shared Child Safety Engine implements BS EN 13120 compliance (MCD-01A §5.6). "Installation height" and "Customer refusal workflow" are shared responsibilities of that engine and are not restated here.

Roller-specific implementation is limited to:
- **Chain safety device mapping** — which device (e.g. breakaway connector, chain guide, tensioner) applies to Standard vs. Cassette Construction Variant (§3), since the two constructions mount the chain differently.
- **Chain length calculation basis** — chain length is drop-derived (longer drop requires a longer chain to reach a safe/usable operating position). **Not yet specified:** the actual formula or supplier-defined ratio — needs supplier/business data before technical design.
- **Breakaway device compatibility** with the selected chain safety device and Construction Variant.

## 13. Pricing Inputs

Pricing calculations are provided by the shared Pricing Engine. Roller-specific pricing parameters include:
- Width
- Drop
- Fabric Collection
- Fabric Band
- Construction Variant
- Fascia
- Bottom Bar
- Motor
- Brackets
- Accessories
- Supplier

Commercial pricing behaviour is defined by the shared Pricing Engine.

**Customer-Facing Documentation Rule acknowledgment:** no Roller-specific exception exists to MCD-01A §5.7/§5.11 — quotations and invoices for Roller Blinds show total price only, with the full itemised breakdown (fabric band, dimensions, construction, accessories) confined to the internal working view.

## 14. Recommendation Rules

Recommendations are provided by the shared Recommendation Engine. Roller-specific recommendations may include:
- Blackout fabric for bedrooms
- Screen fabric for offices
- Cassette systems where aesthetics require
- Reverse roll where window handles interfere
- Battery motors where mains power is unavailable
- Mains motors for frequent operation
- Alternative suppliers where lead time or capability is improved

Recommendations shall explain the reason for the recommendation. Recommendations shall never override advisor decisions.

## 15. Supplier Capability Requirements

Supplier documents shall define: available constructions, fabric collections, colourways, maximum dimensions, tube availability, motor compatibility, cassette availability, accessories, manufacturing limits, lead times, discontinued products, product availability.

Supplier capability shall remain independent of Product Engine behaviour.

## 16. Future Enhancements

Reserved for future functionality including: linked blinds, double roller systems, smart sensor integration, Matter compatibility, AI optimisation, automated fabric recommendations.
