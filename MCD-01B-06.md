## Documentation Conventions: Advisor Selection / Derived Default / Supplier Capability

Consistent with MCD-01B-02's, MCD-01B-03's, MCD-01B-04's, and MCD-01B-05's Documentation Conventions sections, the following three terms are used throughout this document as **documentation conventions only** — they are not new architectural layers and do not modify MCD-01A §3:

- **Advisor Selection** — a value the advisor actively chooses. Architecturally, this is the Configuration layer (or, where the choice changes construction, the Construction/System layer).
- **Derived Default** — a value the system determines automatically from an Advisor Selection, without a separate question. Architecturally, this is the Accessory Engine and/or Supplier Capability Engine resolving a compatible component or value from an existing Configuration selection.
- **Supplier Capability** — the actual data (dimensions, weights, compatibility, pricing, availability) that makes a Derived Default resolvable. Architecturally, this is the Supplier Capability Engine (MCD-01A §5.9) and the Supplier Capability Library, maintained in MCD-04.

## Supplier-Independence Statement

The design brief itself never named the two suppliers whose evidence it describes — it refers to them throughout only as "one supplier" and "the other supplier" — so no supplier-neutrality risk of the kind found and removed in MCD-01B-05 (former §41) arose in drafting this document; there was nothing to accidentally embed. 1. Purpose
This document defines the supplier-neutral product specification for Metal Venetian Blinds within Solara OS.
It establishes: the shared product structure; construction-grouping rules driven by slat size and tilt mechanism; specialist-construction classification; advisor-facing configuration fields; survey and validation requirements; manual and motorised operation models, including how asymmetric motorisation evidence across supplier routes is represented; child-safety behaviour; recommendation logic; accessory handling; document-generation rules; supplier-capability dependencies.
Supplier-specific ranges, dimensions, colours, motor models, and manufacturing restrictions remain within MCD-04 — Product Library & Supplier Management.
This specification must not encode one supplier's technical rules as universal Metal Venetian behaviour.

2. Product definition
A Metal Venetian Blind is a made-to-measure internal blind comprising: a headrail; horizontal aluminium slats; a tilt mechanism (wand, cord, or a specialist mechanism per §6); a matching or coordinated bottom bar; manual or supported motorised controls; installation brackets or, for specialist window-fit constructions, a guide-based mounting system; applicable child-safety devices.
The product provides adjustable privacy and light control through slat tilting and, on constructions that support it, may be fully raised or lowered.

3. Product-family position
Standard Blinds -> Metal Venetian Blinds
Metal Venetian Blinds remain separate from: Wooden Venetian Blinds; Faux Wood Venetian Blinds; Perfect Fit Metal Venetian Blinds; shutters.
Perfect Fit is a separate construction-system family and must not appear as a sub-configuration within this standard product — see §36 for the full exclusions statement and a note on this family's current status in MCD-01A's own taxonomy.

4. Relationship to the Venetian Reference Implementation
This product inherits the following concepts from MCD-01B-04, the ratified Venetian-family reference implementation: Recess and Exact measurement; control-side arrangement; bracket allocation; valance or pelmet treatment where applicable; stack height; hold-down brackets; bay-window grouping; alignment; child-safety validation; the motorisation eligibility model (Manual / Motorised — Tilt Only / Motorised — Tilt and Lift, mapped onto MCD-01A's four existing Operation Engine modes); supplier-capability filtering; internal and customer-facing document rules.
This document overrides or extends the reference implementation only where Metal Venetian behaves differently. The most significant differences, documented in their own sections rather than duplicated from MCD-01B-04: a slat-size-driven Construction Variant split with a tilt mechanism (wand vs. cord) tied to slat size rather than freely advisor-selectable (§6); four specialist mechanical constructions with their own eligibility and mutual-exclusivity rules (§6, §12); a colour-level Mixed Colours configuration unique to this product (§10); and an asymmetric, two-tier motorisation evidence model required by genuinely uneven supplier evidence (§14) — Wooden Venetian's §15 documents a single evidence-symmetric model that does not need this distinction.

5. Design principles
Advisor-first. Supplier-neutral. Capability-driven. Non-assumptive. Advisory rather than restrictive.

6. Construction Variants — slat size, tilt mechanism, and specialist mechanisms

This section states the conclusions reached in the Decision Register (Required Classification Questions 1 and 2); it does not re-derive them — see the Decision Register for the full three-test reasoning behind every row below.

Metal Venetian Blinds comprise **six Construction Variants**, each passing MCD-01A §4's Construction/System test (a change to headrail, bracket, assembly, or mechanism route):

| Construction Variant | Tilt/lift mechanism | Slat-size eligibility | Mounting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Narrow-Slat (Wand) | Wand-operated tilt | The narrower slat-size family (Configuration-layer selection within this construction — see below) | Standard bracket, slimmer headrail |
| Wide-Slat (Cord) | Cord-operated tilt | The wider slat-size family (Configuration-layer selection within this construction — see below) | Standard bracket, deeper/larger headrail |
| Side Guide Tilt | Wand-family tilt, guide-constrained | Narrow slat sizes only | Guide-based mounting for windows that tilt open; all controls same side |
| Side Guide Roof | Wand-family tilt, guide-wire-constrained | The larger narrow slat size only | Guide-wire mounting for roof/skylight windows; all controls same side |
| Partition | A distinct flexible tilt mechanism; tilt-only, cannot raise or lower | Narrow slat sizes and one wide slat size (not the largest wide size) | Uses whichever headrail platform corresponds to the selected slat size, but a distinct tilt mechanism regardless |
| Double Glazing | A distinct torsion-rod-and-bowden-cable mechanism; full tilt, raise, and lower | Same slat-size eligibility as Partition | Uses whichever headrail platform corresponds to the selected slat size, but a distinct actuation mechanism regardless |

**Slat size as Configuration within Narrow-Slat (Wand) and Wide-Slat (Cord):** within each of these two base constructions, the specific slat size is a mandatory Configuration-layer selection (it must be chosen to correctly manufacture the blind, but does not itself change the construction route already fixed by the Wand/Cord split) — see Decision Register for why slat size is not itself the Construction-defining characteristic.

**Mixed Colours** is a Configuration-layer (Colourway) selection, not a Construction Variant — available only where the larger narrow slat size is the underlying construction, and explicitly incompatible with Side Guide Tilt, Side Guide Roof, Partition, and Double Glazing (a Validation Engine compatibility rule — see §16 and Decision Register).

Real dimensional envelopes, exact slat-size millimetre values, and specific mechanism eligibility per construction are Supplier Capability facts — see MCD-04. This section states only the structural classification; it does not embed supplier-specific figures.

7. Core advisor workflow
Room; Window reference; Fit type; Width; Drop; Construction Variant (resolved from window type and, where relevant, glazing type — see §6); Slat size, where the selected Construction Variant offers more than one; Colour, including Mixed Colours where eligible; Manual or motorised operation; Control arrangement, where the Construction Variant permits advisor choice; Brackets or guide-mounting requirements; Hold-down brackets, where applicable; Installation height; Child-safety validation; Supplier capability resolution; Price calculation; Internal survey confirmation.

8. Measurement mode
8.1 Metal Venetian Blinds support Recess and Exact. The selected mode must be stored explicitly.
8.2 Recess: advisor enters measured recess width and drop; supplier-specific deductions applied automatically; customer-facing quotation must not display measured width, measured drop, supplier deductions, manufacturing size.
8.3 Exact: advisor enters required finished blind size; system must not apply a recess deduction.
8.4 Measurement validation (Survey Engine): width; drop; installation height; recess depth; obstructions; window opening type (fixed, tilt-opening, roof/skylight, double-glazed — determines Construction Variant eligibility per §6); fitting surface; multiple-blind alignment; bay-window relationships.

9. Recess suitability and clearance
Metal Venetian Blinds require sufficient recess depth for the selected Construction Variant's headrail and mounting system. Wide-Slat (Cord) constructions use deeper/larger headrails than Narrow-Slat (Wand) constructions per supplier evidence, so recess suitability must be assessed per Construction Variant, not as one Metal Venetian-wide clearance figure. This is recommendation and validation logic, not a universal prohibition.

10. Colour and finish
Colour record must support: active; discontinued; temporarily unavailable; limited stock; remake or legacy only; supplier confirmation required — consistent with MCD-01B-04/05's model.
**Mixed Colours** (§6): a distinct colour per slat within one blind, available only where the larger narrow slat size is the underlying construction, and explicitly incompatible with the four specialist mechanical constructions (Side Guide Tilt, Side Guide Roof, Partition, Double Glazing). This is a mutual-exclusivity rule the Validation Engine must enforce (§16), not merely a display filter.

11. Manual operation
11.1 Narrow-Slat (Wand) construction: tilt is wand-operated. Control-side arrangement remains an advisor-facing Configuration selection where the supplier permits more than one valid arrangement.
11.2 Wide-Slat (Cord) construction: tilt is cord-operated. No wand option exists for this construction per supplier evidence, corroborated independently across two suppliers — this is not modelled as an advisor choice between wand and cord within one construction, since the mechanism is fixed by which Construction Variant (and therefore which slat size) was selected, not freely chosen afterward.
11.3 Side Guide Tilt, Side Guide Roof, Partition, and Double Glazing each carry their own control-arrangement rules (e.g. "all controls same side" for the two Side Guide constructions and for Double Glazing) — Supplier Capability values, not shared Metal Venetian rules; the Product Builder must apply the exact rule for the selected Construction Variant rather than a single Metal Venetian-wide control model.

12. Specialist constructions — additional detail
12.1 Side Guide Tilt: for windows that tilt open. All controls fitted on the same side. Available only on narrow slat sizes (§6). The guide-based mounting is a required part of this Construction Variant, not an optional accessory.
12.2 Side Guide Roof: for roof/skylight windows. A guide wire holds the slats at the required angle. All controls fitted on the same side. Available only on the larger narrow slat size (§6).
12.3 Partition: uses a distinct flexible tilt mechanism. Functionally restricted to tilt only — it cannot raise or lower. This is a genuine, permanent functional characteristic of the construction, not a reduced-capability tier of the standard product, and must not be presented to the advisor as if lift capability might later become available on the same physical build. Available on narrow slat sizes and one wide slat size (not the largest wide size) — see §6.
12.4 Double Glazing: uses a distinct torsion-rod-and-bowden-cable mechanism. Supports full tilt, raise, and lower, unlike Partition. All controls fitted on the same side. Same slat-size eligibility as Partition (§6). The Product Builder must not assume Double Glazing shares Partition's tilt-only restriction merely because they share the same slat-size eligibility — the two constructions differ in both mechanism and functional capability, and are recorded as fully separate Construction Variants (§6, Decision Register).

13. Width, drop, area and weight validation
Validation Engine must evaluate: minimum/maximum width and drop per Construction Variant (each of the six carries its own envelope — see MCD-04); maximum area where supplier evidence provides it; slat-size eligibility within Narrow-Slat (Wand) and Wide-Slat (Cord); the Mixed Colours mutual-exclusivity rule against the four specialist constructions (§6, §10); motorised versus manual eligibility per Construction Variant and, for motorised configurations, per the evidence/quoting distinction in §14. A simple maximum-width field is insufficient — capability must be expressed per Construction Variant, and, within Narrow-Slat (Wand) and Wide-Slat (Cord), per slat size.

14. Motorisation

14.1 Shared model: the shared Operation Engine supports Manual, Motorised — Tilt Only, and Motorised — Tilt and Lift, mapped onto MCD-01A's four existing Operation Engine modes, consistent with MCD-01B-04/05.

14.2 Evidence asymmetry — represented precisely, not smoothed over. Motorisation evidence for Metal Venetian is genuinely uneven across supplier routes, and this specification represents that unevenness using two distinct fields rather than collapsing it into a single label:

- **Evidence Status** — what is actually known about whether motorisation exists for a given configuration.
- **Quoting Status** — whether the advisor may act on it (enable it for quoting).

| Evidence state | Meaning | Quoting |
|---|---|---|
| Available | Complete capability evidence supports the configuration | Enabled |
| Not Available | Supplier explicitly prohibits it | Disabled |
| Not Confirmed | No reliable evidence either way | Disabled |
| Category-Level Confirmed | Supplier confirms the product category supports motorisation, but eligible construction, slat size, and technical limits are missing | Disabled |

14.3 A supplier route may confirm, at the level of its general technical documentation, that Metal Venetian (alongside other product categories) is a supported motorised category, without that route's documentation specifying which Construction Variant, slat size, motor model, torque, voltage, or width/drop limits apply. This is recorded as **Evidence Status: Category-Level Availability Confirmed / Quoting Status: Disabled — Slat-Size Capability Not Provided**. This state must never be displayed or treated as "motorisation not available" for that route — the category-level confirmation is real evidence, not an absence of evidence — and it must never be treated as sufficient to enable quoting for any specific slat size or Construction Variant on that route. The two states are independent: evidence confidence does not by itself change quoting eligibility, and quoting eligibility is never inferred from evidence alone.

14.4 The system must not infer motor model, torque, size limits, or slat-size eligibility for a category-level-only route from another route's fully-evidenced figures, from component-code similarity, or from the general category-level confirmation itself. Any such inference is expressly prohibited, per Decision Register and Open Evidence Items.

14.5 Hard restrictions, where confirmed rather than merely unconfirmed, are recorded as **Evidence Status: Not Available** (not "Not Confirmed") and remain permanently disabled for quoting regardless of future evidence gathering on other routes — e.g. a specific wide-slat size confirmed by supplier evidence as never motorisable under any circumstance.

15. Motorised construction restrictions
Product Builder must support restrictions such as: motorisation limited to specific slat sizes; taped or corded operation limited to Tilt Only; a specific slat size confirmed as never motorisable; minimum motorised width per operation mode; battery-only or mains-only availability per Construction Variant. These are Supplier Capability values, not shared Metal Venetian rules, and must be evaluated per Construction Variant and, within Narrow-Slat (Wand)/Wide-Slat (Cord), per slat size.

16. Compatibility and mutual exclusivity
The Validation Engine must enforce: slat-size eligibility within Narrow-Slat (Wand) and Wide-Slat (Cord) (§6); Construction Variant eligibility for Mixed Colours (§10); the mutual exclusivity of Mixed Colours with Side Guide Tilt, Side Guide Roof, Partition, and Double Glazing (§6, §10); Construction Variant eligibility for motorisation per §14's evidence/quoting model. This is the same shape of compatibility enforcement already established at Configured for MCD-01B-03's Same Room fabric/colourway mismatch rule and MCD-01B-05's colour-specific-limit-overrides-range-limit precedence rule — see Decision Register.

17. Headrail
Headrail properties remain supplier-specific and vary by Construction Variant: width; height; depth; material; colour; reinforcement. Narrow-Slat (Wand) uses a slimmer headrail than Wide-Slat (Cord) per supplier evidence. Customer-facing interface does not need to expose headrail dimensions unless relevant to suitability.

18. Valance or pelmet
Where a Construction Variant supports a valance or pelmet treatment, the Product Builder follows the same Recess/Exact and attachment-method model established in MCD-01B-04 §19, applied per Construction Variant. Availability and profile remain supplier-specific.

19. Bottom bar
May vary by Construction Variant and slat size. Possible profiles remain supplier-specific. Capability record must support profile, material, dimensions, colour, end caps or buttons where applicable.

20. Brackets and guide mounting
20.1 Narrow-Slat (Wand) and Wide-Slat (Cord): standard bracket-mounted, consistent with MCD-01B-04's shared bracket model (top fix, face fix, end fix, and similar, per supplier range).
20.2 Side Guide Tilt and Side Guide Roof: guide-based mounting is a required part of the construction, not an optional accessory — see §12.1, §12.2. The system must not offer a standard-bracket alternative for these two constructions.
20.3 Bracket or guide-mounting quantity may depend on width, headrail reinforcement, motorisation, and the specific Construction Variant's own supplier table — there is no universal Metal Venetian bracket formula.

21. Hold-down brackets
May be recommended for doors, inward-opening windows, frequently moving panels, locations where the blind may swing — consistent with MCD-01B-04's model, applied where the selected Construction Variant supports it.

22. Stack height
Depends on drop, slat size, Construction Variant, and mechanism. Product Builder may display an estimated stack height where supplier data exists, labelled as approximate.

23. Bay windows and multiple blinds
Bay-window configurations require separate blind items unless a supplier-specific combined solution exists, consistent with MCD-01B-04's model. Any curved or specialist system beyond the six Construction Variants defined in §6 must be treated as a separate supplier capability, not assumed within this specification.

24. Child safety
Mandatory, handled through the shared Child Safety Engine, consistent with MCD-01B-04 §25. Safety-device mapping (chain, cord, or wand-specific requirements) must be resolved per Construction Variant, since the underlying control mechanism differs between Narrow-Slat (Wand), Wide-Slat (Cord), and the four specialist constructions.

25. Installation height
Required field for corded manual Metal Venetian Blinds (Wide-Slat (Cord) construction, and any specialist construction using cord-based control). System must use it to determine maximum permitted control length, cleat position, floor clearance, and Construction-Variant-specific control reduction. The system must never invent or approximate a control length.

26. Recommendation Engine
May surface guidance on window-opening type driving Construction Variant eligibility (§6), recess suitability per Construction Variant (§9), and motorisation availability given the evidence/quoting distinction in §14 — the advisor must never be led to believe a Category-Level-Confirmed route is simply "not motorised." Advisor retains final control.

27. Accessories
Guide wires and guide-track components for Side Guide Tilt and Side Guide Roof (required parts of those constructions per §20.2, not general accessories); replacement slats; remote controls; wall switches; chargers; hubs; motor accessories where the Construction Variant supports motorisation. Availability and pricing remain supplier-specific.

28. Pricing behaviour
May depend on Construction Variant, slat size, colour (including any Mixed Colours surcharge), motorisation, accessories, supplier route, and commercial route (§30). Pricing Engine must resolve the cheapest valid supplier route only after capability validation, including quoting-status validation for motorisation (§14). Price must not override technical suitability.

29. Supplier selection and commercial route
29.1 Where one supplier route resells another route's technical content (confirmed by matching rules, wording, and surcharges, even referencing slat sizes the reselling route does not itself stock, alongside genuinely different prices), technical capability belongs to the original route; price, lead time, and ordering belong to the reselling route. The product's technical capability must not be duplicated as if it were a separate manufactured range — see MCD-04 §7.11 and Decision Register.
29.2 Supplier identity may remain internal unless disclosure adds customer value, consistent with MCD-01B-04/05.

30. Discontinued and limited-stock items
Consistent with MCD-01B-04/05's model: Active; Temporarily Unavailable; Discontinued; Limited Stock; Legacy / Remake Only; Supplier Confirmation Required. A discontinued Construction Variant, slat size, or colour must not be presented to a customer merely because a supplier portal still allows selection.

31. Duplicate-item behaviour
Advisor may duplicate a Metal Venetian item for another window. System must clearly highlight differences in room, window reference, Construction Variant, width, drop, slat size, colour, operation, controls, brackets or guide mounting, installation height, accessories. Any deviation from a linked alignment group must trigger confirmation.

32. Internal versus customer-facing data
Internal view may include measurements, deductions, supplier route, Construction Variant, motor, controls, brackets or guide mounting, installation height, child-safety calculations, production notes, price breakdown, cost, margin.
Customer quotation must include room or location, Metal Venetian Blind, colour or finish, manual or motorised operation, relevant visible options, total price.
Customer documents must not display measurements, deductions, supplier trade terminology, cost price, internal margin, technical motor calculations.

33. Survey confirmation
Before order release, advisor must confirm: measurements checked; window opening type confirmed (fixed, tilt-opening, roof/skylight, or double-glazed, driving Construction Variant per §6); recess depth suitable for the selected Construction Variant; colour confirmed, including Mixed Colours eligibility where selected; controls confirmed; motorisation confirmed where selected, respecting the evidence/quoting distinction (§14); installation height recorded; child-safety method confirmed; bracket or guide-mounting type confirmed; alignment instructions recorded; customer approval obtained.

34. Validation severity
Hard stop: size exceeds confirmed Construction-Variant capability; motorisation quoting-disabled configuration selected for quoting; Mixed Colours selected with an incompatible specialist construction; child safety cannot be achieved; selected bracket or guide-mounting type incompatible with the Construction Variant; required installation data missing.
Warning: shallow recess may obstruct operation for the selected Construction Variant's headrail depth; large blind may be heavy to operate manually; alignment requested but cannot be guaranteed.
Information: default control arrangement; supplier-selected motor where applicable; approximate stack height; expected lead time.

35. Supplier Capability schema requirements
Each Metal Venetian capability record should support: Supplier; Original Product Owner; Ordering Supplier; Commercial Route; Construction Variant; Slat Size; Colour; Colour Status; Minimum Width; Maximum Width; Minimum Drop; Maximum Drop; Maximum Area; Mechanism Type; Control Arrangement; Tilt-Only Motorisation; Tilt-and-Lift Motorisation; Motor Power Type; Motor Protocol; Motor Size Limits; Bracket or Guide-Mounting Compatibility; Headrail; Bottom Bar; Stack Height; Recess Deduction; Manufacturing Tolerance; Installation Height Rule; Control-Length Formula; Safety Devices; Accessories; Lead Time; Pricing Source; Evidence Status; Quoting Status; Source Reference; Evidence Date; Verification Status.

36. Exclusions
This specification does not include: Wooden Venetian Blinds; Faux Wood Venetian Blinds; Perfect Fit Metal Venetian Blinds; shutters; any product or construction not evidenced in the research behind this document. Perfect Fit Metal Venetian is a separate construction-system family per the standing project decision (the same decision already applied in MCD-01B-04 §3 and MCD-01B-05 §3), not a sub-configuration of this product. **Note:** MCD-01A's own Product Taxonomy (§4) currently lists Perfect Fit Wooden Venetian and Perfect Fit Faux Wood Venetian under Perfect Fit Systems, but does not yet list Perfect Fit Metal Venetian — this specification's exclusion statement does not depend on that taxonomy entry existing, but the gap is noted here rather than silently assumed away, since MCD-01A itself is not modified by this document. ## 38. Decision Register

Maintained in accordance with MCD-00A conventions as established in MCD-01B-01, MCD-01B-02, MCD-01B-03, MCD-01B-04, MCD-01B-05, and MCD-04 (MCD-00A itself was not available in the repository at time of writing — see below). Format: Date | Decision | Reasoning | Raised By.

| Date | Decision | Reasoning | Raised By |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17 Jul 2026 | **Required Classification Question 1 — the narrow-slat (wand) and wide-slat (cord) groups are two separate Construction Variants (Narrow-Slat (Wand) and Wide-Slat (Cord)); slat size within each is a Configuration-layer selection, not itself the Construction-defining characteristic.** Applying MCD-01A §4's three-test model to the wand/cord split itself: **Test 1 (Construction/System)** — does it change the product's construction or manufacturing route (headrail, brackets, assembly, tube/mechanism selection)? The evidence shows narrow slat sizes use slimmer headrails with wand-actuated tilt, while wide slat sizes use deeper/larger headrails with cord-actuated tilt — a simultaneous headrail change and mechanism-selection change, corroborated independently by two unrelated suppliers. This matches MCD-01A §4's own Roller worked example for Cassette almost exactly ("it changes the headrail, bracket design, assembly... and tube selection"). Test 1 passes for the wand/cord split. **Distinguishing this from Vertical's Control Mechanism (Configuration-layer, MCD-01B-02):** the brief itself flags the key difference, and this document confirms it holds under the actual test — Vertical's Control Mechanism was a free advisor choice within one construction, unconstrained by any other physical selection. Here, the tilt mechanism is not independently selectable — it is determined by which slat size the advisor chooses, and that same slat-size choice simultaneously drives headrail, bracket, and dimensional-limit differences. A characteristic that is *coupled* to a construction-defining choice in this way, rather than *itself* being a free advisor choice, is the Construction-layer characteristic, not the Configuration-layer one. **Slat size within each mechanism family**, by contrast, fails Test 1 on its own — the evidence describes two slat sizes per mechanism family (15/16mm and 25mm both wand-operated; 35mm and 50mm both cord-operated) differing in dimensional envelope and colour-range breadth, not in headrail/bracket/mechanism route.
